PROCEDURE

1) HOT MOUNTING












Process to be followed while placing the specimen:

i)Switch on the power button.
ii)Apply antistick stearate powder to the surface of lower ram and upper ram  to avoid sticking of resin.
iii)Place the specimen on the ram with the surface to be viewed facing downwards.
i)Switch on the power button.
Pouring resin over the specimen:

vi)Press and hold the RAM DOWN to the lower the ram to it’s lower limit. 
v)Fill a suitable amount of resin about 1 big scoop into the cylinder through the funnel.
v)Press and hold the RAM DOWN to the lower the ram to it’s lower limit. 
Installing the top closure:

v i)Place top closure with the upper ram on the mounting cylinder.
vii)Place the top closure downward, turn it clockwise until it’s lower limit. 
viii)Turn the top closure a three quarters of the turn back. 

Parameters before starting the mounting process: 

ix)Check the table for correct resin specifications eg: heating time; cooling time. 
x)Press the start button and the process will run automatically.
xi)The machine automatically stops and relieves the pressure when the cooling time has elapsed.
xii)Remove the top closure.
xiii)Turn to closure in counter clock-wise direction until released from the thread.
xiv)Hold the top closure and press RAM up to raise the lower RAM to it’s upper limit. 
xv)Remove the sample.
xvi)Switch off the hot mount press.

2) COLD MOUNTING


i)Switch on the compressor.
ii)Insert tubing into the stopper (ensure enough length for the epoxy to flow into the module).
iii)Place the sample in the mould and place them inside the chamber.
iv)Turn the vacuum knob clockwise to max. 
v)Turn the knob behind the Epovac to the anticlockwise to pump down the chamber.
vi)Place tubing on the metal stand and tighten the knob.
vii)Tighten stopper and knob to pump down to reach about 400m bar.
viii)Allow the vacuum to hold for about five minutes.
ix)Prepare epoxy compound. Get a disposable cup.
x)Fill the syringe with 20ml of epoxy resin. Inject 20ml of resin into the mixing cup.

xi)Inject 5ml of hardener into the cup and stir slowly for about 5 minutes to reduce the formation of bubbles.
xii)Place tubing into the cup and release the knob to allow epoxy to flow.
xiii)Try not to release any bubble into the mould.
ix)After sufficient epoxy is added, tighten the knob to stop the flow of epoxy.
x)Turn the vacuum knob to min and wait for 30 minutes.
xi)Vent the chamber by turning the knob behind the epovac clockwise.
xii)Remove the mould with the sample inside and leave it to harden for 8 hours before removing the cold.
